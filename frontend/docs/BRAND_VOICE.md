# Esperion Brand Voice Guidelines

## Overview

Esperion's brand voice is **Friendly & Approachable**. We speak like a trusted partner, not a corporation. Our tone is casual, welcoming, and human—always focusing on real conversations over corporate jargon.

---

## Core Principles

### 1. Friendly & Approachable
- **Do**: Use contractions (we're, you'll, let's)
- **Do**: Speak directly to the reader using "you"
- **Don't**: Use formal or stiff language
- **Don't**: Sound like a faceless corporation

**Example:**
- ✅ "Let's build something great together"
- ❌ "We facilitate collaborative development initiatives"

### 2. Clear & Direct
- **Do**: Use simple, everyday words
- **Do**: Get to the point quickly
- **Don't**: Use buzzwords or filler language
- **Don't**: Over-explain simple concepts

**Example:**
- ✅ "We build websites that work"
- ❌ "We leverage cutting-edge technologies to deliver best-in-class digital solutions"

### 3. Supportive & Encouraging
- **Do**: Acknowledge challenges without being negative
- **Do**: Focus on solutions and possibilities
- **Don't**: Use fear-based marketing
- **Don't**: Talk down to readers

**Example:**
- ✅ "Ready for your next digital step? We're here to help."
- ❌ "Don't fall behind your competitors"

### 4. Human & Real
- **Do**: Write like you speak
- **Do**: Show personality
- **Don't**: Use excessive exclamation points!!!
- **Don't**: Force humor or slang

**Example:**
- ✅ "Small team, real impact"
- ❌ "We're a dynamic synergistic thought-leadership powerhouse!!!"

## Tone by Context

### Homepage & Landing Pages
**Tone**: Welcoming, confident, action-oriented

**Example:**
> "Transforming businesses through innovative digital solutions. Let's build your next project together."

### Service Descriptions
**Tone**: Helpful, informative, focused on benefits

**Example:**
> "Web Development that Works. We build fast, reliable websites that help your business grow."

### Blog & Articles
**Tone**: Educational, friendly, conversational

**Example:**
> "Here's what we learned building 150+ websites. Let's dive in."

### Contact & CTAs
**Tone**: Inviting, low-pressure, supportive

**Example:**
> "Got a project in mind? Let's chat. No pressure, just a friendly conversation about what you need."

### Error Messages
**Tone**: Apologetic, helpful, human

**Example:**
> "Oops! Something went wrong. Let's try that again."

## Writing Tips

### 1. Start with "You"
Focus on the reader's needs, not our features.

**Instead of:**
> "We offer comprehensive digital marketing services"

**Write:**
> "You'll get digital marketing that actually works"

### 2. Use Active Voice
Active voice is clearer and more engaging.

**Instead of:**
> "Projects are delivered on time by our team"

**Write:**
> "We deliver projects on time"

### 3. Keep Sentences Short
Break long sentences into shorter ones.

**Instead of:**
> "Our team of experienced professionals will work with you to develop a customized solution that meets your specific business needs"

**Write:**
> "Our team will work with you. We'll build a solution that fits your business."

### 4. Be Specific
Vague claims feel corporate. Specific details feel real.

**Instead of:**
> "We deliver exceptional results"

**Write:**
> "We've completed 150+ projects for growing businesses"

## Examples in Practice

### Homepage Hero
```
✅ Good:
"Ready for Your Next Growth Phase?
Digital solutions for your next growth phase. From planning to launch, we help your team release digital experiences that matter."

❌ Bad:
"Maximize Your Digital Potential
We leverage synergistic methodologies to deliver best-in-class digital transformation solutions."
```

### Service Card
```
✅ Good:
"Web Development
We build fast, reliable websites that help your business grow. From landing pages to complex web apps."

❌ Bad:
"Web Development Solutions
Enterprise-grade web development services utilizing cutting-edge frameworks for optimal digital presence."
```

### Contact Form
```
✅ Good:
"Let's Talk
Tell us about your project. We'll get back to you within 24 hours."

❌ Bad:
"Submit Your Inquiry
Please complete the form below. A representative will contact you within one business day."
```

### About Page
```
✅ Good:
"Small Team, Real Impact
We're a team of strategists, designers, and developers. We work together to make your digital projects successful."

❌ Bad:
"Corporate Excellence
Our organization comprises highly-skilled professionals dedicated to delivering unparalleled service excellence."
```

## Accessibility & Inclusivity

### Use Inclusive Language
- Avoid gendered language (use "they" instead of "he/she")
- Avoid ableist language (use "click" instead of "see", "hear")
- Respect cultural differences

### Write for Everyone
- Use plain language (aim for 8th-grade reading level)
- Explain technical terms when necessary
- Use descriptive link text ("Read our guide" not "Click here")

---

## Brand Voice Checklist

Before publishing, ask:
- [ ] Does this sound like a real person talking?
- [ ] Is it clear and easy to understand?
- [ ] Does it focus on the reader's needs?
- [ ] Are we avoiding corporate jargon?
- [ ] Is the tone friendly and welcoming?
- [ ] Would I say this out loud to a colleague?

## Quick Reference

### Do's
✅ Use contractions (we're, you'll, let's)
✅ Write in active voice
✅ Focus on "you" (the reader)
✅ Keep sentences short and clear
✅ Show personality
✅ Be specific and concrete

### Don'ts
❌ Use corporate buzzwords
❌ Write in passive voice
❌ Sound formal or stiff
❌ Use long, complex sentences
❌ Make vague claims
❌ Use excessive exclamation points
